Your Ultimate Guide to Cognitive Neuroscience

Cognitive Neuroscience is an integration of Cognitive Science and Neuroscience, studying how cognitive functions such as language or memory emerge from neural activity of the brain. There is some ongoing debate about how the brain is organized: some argue for specific processing regions, while others suggest contributions to a distributed process. Methods such as lesion studies, neural activity recordings, brain imaging, and computational models are used to study these processes. These methods, developed in the 1980s and 1990s, continue to deepen our understanding of how brain signals shape perception, memory, and reasoning, revealing insights into how knowledge is represented and utilized in the brain for human thinking, language, and behavior. The aim of this page is to function as an educational space about cognitive neuroscience, and offer up-to-date information about ongoing cognitive neuroscience research projects at the University of Helsinki.
